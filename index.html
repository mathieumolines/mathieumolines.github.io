Mathieu Molines

Associate Professor in Public Management

Hi!

I am an Associate Professor in Public Management at KEDGE Business School. I got a PhD from Toulouse 1 Capitole University in France. My research examines how individuals navigate ethical tensions in public organizations, with a particular focus on high-stakes contexts such as policing and firefighting.

Drawing on social and moral psychology, I study the micro-foundations of behavior in public service, investigating how mechanisms such as moral disengagement and prosocial motivation shape ethical and unethical actions at work. A central goal of my research is to understand how organizational environments and everyday interactions — such as leadership, citizen behavior, or institutional framing — transform how individuals interpret and respond to morally complex situations.

A second strand of my research brings postcolonial perspectives to bear on public administration and street-level bureaucracy. This work explores how colonial legacies continue to shape the conditions under which frontline public servants exercise discretion, contributing to a more historically and politically situated understanding of bureaucratic practice.

More broadly, my work contributes to research on public service motivation, ethical behavior, and organizational dynamics in public settings. I am particularly interested in how psychological processes interact with institutional contexts to produce variation in engagement, decision-making, and conduct on the frontlines of public service.

Alongside my academic work, I also conduct consultancy in leadership and psychometric assessment. I work with organizations using evidence-based tools — including Hogan Assessments — to support leadership development and better understand individual differences in workplace behavior.

I teach courses in team management, leadership, and organizational behavior at the master's level.
